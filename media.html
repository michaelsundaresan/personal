Q and A

Q: Who did you listen to growing up? Who were your favorite bands?

A: I remember being 8 or so and "Ride the Lightning" came out. It
blew my mind. My older brother got me into Megadeth, and I really
liked the raw emotion of the "Peace Sells" album and "So Far..So
Good..So What!". Rust in Peace and Holy Wars were my favorite songs
in 7th grade, I remember it to this day.

Then 1990 came around and I heard "Seasons in the Abyss" on
Headbanger's Ball. That was revolutionary for me. The riffs were so
sharp, and the solos are phenomenal, Hanneman's especially.

Q: What is the name of your project?

A: "Bitter Revelations".

Q: Why that title?

A: It sums up the initial stages of the spiritual path I have followed
in life. Not all truth is beauty. Earlier in life, I thought that
life is futile, religion questionable, and God at best was like the
Great Clock Maker, designing the universe and its constituents and
setting in perpetual motion the wheels of time.

Q: Negative thoughts.

A: Well mostly negative. I think the concept of God being the Great
Clock Maker is neutral.

Q: Where do you stand this day on your feeling of a Higher Power?

A: I believe in God the Creator of the Universe, the sustaining force,
and that also within every man and woman is a spark of the divine fire.
